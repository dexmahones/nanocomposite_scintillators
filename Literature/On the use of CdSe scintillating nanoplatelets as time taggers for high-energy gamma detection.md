In this study, [[Nanoparticle#Core-crown|core-crown]] CdSe/CdS nanoplatelets (NPLs) are used in two scintillator designs. In the first, NPLs are incorporated into a more traditional LYSO scintillator to improve timing response, while in the second the NPLs are imbedded directly in a polystyrene (PS) matrix to eliminate the high cost associated with the LYSO crystal. While the study is primarily focused on the fast response of the NPLs, the reasoning behind this is for the direct application to enhanced time resolution for medical imaging. Before studying the more complex materials, the authors begin by characterizing the luminescence properties of the NPLs under laser and X-ray excitation... 

As a preliminary step toward fabricating the polystyrene-based nanocomposite, NPLs are first drop-cast onto a traditional LYSO crystal. This ensures the high interaction cross section with 511 keV gamma rays needed to examine the response time of the NPLs before embedding them in a matrix with a much lower stopping power.

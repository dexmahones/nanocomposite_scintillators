The authors fabricate a nanocomposite plastic scintillator consisting of LaxCe1-xF3 nanoparticles capped with a polymerizable surfactant for optimal dispersion in a polystyrene matrix. Subsequent characterization of the nanocomposite scintillator includes a measurement of the transmission, emission and excitation spectra, as well as pulse height spectra under 122 keV and 662 keV gamma ray excitation. The authors observe distinct peaks in the pulse height spectra for the nanocomposite material that are absent in those of a nanoparticle-free control, and attribute these features to an increased photoelectric cross section caused by the presence of the nanoparticles. The peak in the 122 keV spectrum can be directly attributed to photoelectric interactions, however that of the 622 keV spectrum is identified as the escape peak of La and Ce K$\alpha$ X-rays. Resolving this into a true photopeak would require further enhancements in transparency, as well as a larger scintillator sample. A brief discussion of scintillation response time is included to verify the negligible effect of nanoparticles on scintillation decay time, however without a thorough description of the instrumentation, this measurement is somewhat dubious. Finally, the authors quote a monetary cost associated with the fabrication of their nanocomposite plastic: the total material costs come to $2.7. 
# Characterization Methods
- XRD - Examine phase structure (hexagonal) of the nanoparticles and estimate their size (11.7 nm).
- TEM - Verify nanoparticle size (12 nm) and examine particle size distribution (uniform). 
- FTIR - Confirm HVDAC (surfactant) coating on nanoparticle surface.
- TGA - Determine content of HVDAC on nanoparticle surface, as well as nanoparticle content in polystyrene monolith.
- Photoluminescence - Emission-excitation wavelength of nanoparticles to see Stoke's shift
	- PL and absorption for various nanocomposite components to examine spectral overlaps and infer energy transitions
	- Enhanced PL due to increased NP composition
- Pulse height spectra - Examine energy resolution at different gamma radiation energies.
	- Co-57 (122 keV gamma rays)
	- Cs-137 (622 keV gamma rays)
- Decay time

# Suspect points
- TEM image portrays distinctly non-uniform nanoparticle dispersion
- Nanoparticle emission peak is at 320 nm
- Photopeak in 122 keV spectrum is very broad, and very wide - poor energy resolution. 
- Escape peak in 622 keV spectrum is indistinct from Compton edge - again, poor energy resolution.
- Decay time measurement not described at all, and may not be relevant or valid, particularly since it has units of mV on the y-axis, indicating that these are just sample pulses?!?
## Pixel fabrication
>[!quote] In the first step, the styrene received from Sigma-Aldrich was run through a silica column to remove the inhibitor. To fabricate PPO–POPOP–PS nanocomposite, the desired amount of PPO (1 wt%) and POPOP (0.05 wt%), 20 L Luperox 231, and styrene were added to a glass vial and ultra-sonicated for 10–15 mins. Luperox 231 was added as an initiator for the thermally initiated freeradical bulk polymerization process. The glass vials were then incubated at 65–70 °C for 3 d. Finally, the samples can be obtained by cooling down to room temperature and removing the samples from the glass vials. To fabricate $La_xCe_{1-x}F_3$–PPO–POPOP nanocomposites, everything is similar except that HVDAC-coated $La_xCe_{1-x}F_3$ nanoparticles were added along with PPO and POPOP.
